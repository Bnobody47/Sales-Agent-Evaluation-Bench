# Audit Memo (Act I)

Public benchmarks such as tau2-bench retail under-measure the exact behaviors that determine whether Tenacious outreach is commercially safe. They primarily score task completion style outcomes, but Tenacious needs benchmark dimensions tied to sales trust, capacity realism, and brand voice. Week 10 evidence shows these are not edge details; they are dominant failure channels with direct pipeline cost.

The highest-risk gap is bench over-commitment. In Week 10 probes (`P-007`, `P-008`, `P-009`), the agent frequently promised staffing timelines and stack support that should have triggered phased-ramp or handoff behavior. This is not a generic language-quality issue; it is an operational truthfulness issue unique to delivery-backed sales motions. A benchmark that only asks whether outreach is fluent can mark these outputs as good while they are business-invalid. This is why Tenacious-Bench treats bench-to-brief consistency as a first-class score component.

The second gap is groundedness under sparse signal. Week 10 probes (`P-004`, `P-005`, `P-006`, `P-028`, `P-029`, `P-030`) show over-claiming behavior when public evidence is weak. Generic benchmark tasks rarely encode evidence availability constraints; they evaluate plausibility. Tenacious requires evidence-linked language: if data is missing, the model must ask rather than assert. The benchmark therefore encodes explicit input fields for signal confidence and checks claim structure against those fields.

The third gap is style-guide fidelity under pressure. The Tenacious style guide requires outputs to remain direct, grounded, honest, professional, and non-condescending across objections and re-engagement. Week 10 probes (`P-010`, `P-011`, `P-012`) show measurable drift when conversations turn defensive. Public benchmarks mostly treat tone as optional or binary toxicity checks. Tenacious-Bench instead uses marker-level tone scoring and hard failures for phrases that trigger "offshore vendor" skepticism.

The fourth gap is dual-control and channel-state behavior (`P-019`, `P-020`, `P-021`, `P-022`, `P-023`, `P-024`). Generic benchmarks usually collapse interaction into single-turn output quality. The real sales workflow includes stateful constraints: do not push booking when a question was asked, do not lose timezone correctness, and preserve channel hierarchy (email-first, warm SMS follow-up). These errors are conversion-critical even when message text is otherwise acceptable.

A fifth Tenacious-specific gap is cross-thread integrity (`P-013`, `P-014`, `P-015`). In B2B outreach, leaking details between contacts or channels creates immediate trust damage and compliance risk. Most public benchmark tasks do not model thread-level context isolation, so they cannot penalize this class of defect.

Together, these gaps justify a domain benchmark with machine-verifiable checks tied to Tenacious constraints rather than generic retail agent success. The new schema must include: bench availability, signal confidence, segment context, prior-thread state, and policy markers. Outputs are graded on weighted dimensions with deterministic rule checks plus calibrated judge scoring where language interpretation is unavoidable. The chosen focus for method training is bench over-commitment because it combines substantial trigger rate with the highest modeled business impact and clear mechanism-level interventions.
